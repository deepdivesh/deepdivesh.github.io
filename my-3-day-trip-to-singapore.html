My 3 day trip to Singapore

19 Jun 2017 on random

I visited Singapore last year and went back home after just 3 days. That might not seem crazy to you, but it should after I tell you that Singapore is 12,000 kilometers away from my hometown in Croatia. ðŸ™ˆ

This happened because of one of my biggest shortcomings - saying yes too much. I accepted traveling to Singapore and staying there for a week in order to get to know the project and the team I'm gonna be working with.

I didn't know anything about the project, except that it's a fintech startup. This raised red flags to my friend Dino, who warned me that it might not be a good project and that I can find better. My reaction was "Meh, how bad can it be?". Turns out it can be pretty bad.

I ended up flying over there not knowing where I am staying until I landed. That was the first red flag to me and seeing that I got accommodated in a room without proper windows was the second. The third was seeing what the project was about. I'm not a fan of fintech and working with numbers too heavily and this project turned out to be a bunch of expandable and collapsable tables. Glorified Excel spreadsheets in its nastiest form.

After realising all this, I started panicking. Combined with that was the jet lag which prevented me from getting any sleep at night. I started thinking about quitting that job and staying there until the return flight they paid for.

I didn't like the idea too much. There are a bunch of reasons for this:

  • I couldn't find an ATM for my crappy Maestro card, so I had a fixed amount of money on me
  • the weather was killing me
  • I didn't like their food
  • I was alone

All things considered, I decided to pay an extra $900 and return home after just 3 days. Asian plane tickets are crazy. I've taken two-way ticket from Croatia for ~ $900 and here I had to pay $900 for just one way.

This was my $900 lesson from Singapore and the main thing I learned is to stop saying yes too much. The second thing I learned is that it's great to be prepared and know your accommodation if you're going on a 12,000km trip.
